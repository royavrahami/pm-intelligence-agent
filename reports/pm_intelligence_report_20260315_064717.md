**Score:** 71 | **Category:** Engineering Leadership

**Summary:** The article compares staff augmentation and managed services as two distinct outsourcing models for software development. Staff augmentation involves hiring developers to integrate into your existing team, where you maintain control over the project management and delivery. Managed services, on the other hand, involve outsourcing the entire project or function to a vendor who manages the team and is responsible for delivering the outcome. Each model has its own cost structures, management responsibilities, and flexibility, making them suitable for different project needs.

**Key Insights:**

- Understanding the cost structures and management responsibilities of each model is crucial to avoid scope creep and misaligned expectations.

**For PM/PgM:** For a Project/Program Manager in a high-tech company, choosing the right outsourcing model is critical to project delivery success. It affects how you manage team performance, control costs, and communicate with stakeholders.
