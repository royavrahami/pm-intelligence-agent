"Just in Time" approach to meetings
★★★★☆ 82 🔄 Agile & Scrum 11 Mar 2026 13:00 UTC
The 'Just in Time' approach to meetings in Scrum emphasizes meeting at the moment collaboration is most valuable, rather than planning everything far in advance. Scrum Teams meet briefly and frequently to inspect progress, coordinate work, and adjust plans, which helps catch problems early. Timeboxing is a key feature of Scrum meetings, ensuring they remain efficient by setting maximum durations without minimums, allowing teams to finish early if objectives are met. This approach reduces overall meeting time while maintaining focus on delivering value.

Improving Effort Estimation Accuracy: Why 3 Hours Becomes 3 Weeks and Solutions
★★★☆☆ 59 10 Mar 2026 15:00 UTC
Effort estimation in software development is often inaccurate due to cognitive biases and unforeseen complexities. Developers frequently underestimate time requirements, with estimates being only 30% of actual time needed. Common biases include the Planning Fallacy, Anchoring Effect, Dunning-Kruger Effect, Hofstadter's Law, and Parkinson's Law. Techniques like 3-Point Estimation (PERT) and Reference Class Forecasting can improve accuracy by considering multiple scenarios and historical data.
🗂️ For PM/PgM: Accurate effort estimation is crucial for project planning and resource allocation in high-tech companies. By understanding and mitigating biases, PMs can improve project timelines, manage stakeholder expectations, and enhance team productivity. Techniques like PERT and Reference Class Forecasting provide structured methods to refine estimates, directly impacting project delivery and strategic planning.
