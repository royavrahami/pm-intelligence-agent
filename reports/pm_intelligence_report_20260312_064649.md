### ["Just in Time" approach to meetings](https://www.scrum.org/resources/blog/just-time-approach-meetings)
**Score:** 82 | **Category:** Agile & Scrum

**Summary:** The 'Just in Time' approach to meetings in Scrum emphasizes meeting at the moment collaboration is most valuable, rather than planning everything far in advance. Scrum Teams meet briefly and frequently to inspect progress, coordinate work, and adjust plans, which helps catch problems early. Timeboxing is a key feature of Scrum meetings, ensuring they remain efficient by setting maximum durations without minimums, allowing teams to finish early if objectives are met. This approach reduces overall meeting time while maintaining focus on delivering value.

**Key Insights:**
- Implement a 'just in time' meeting strategy to ensure meetings occur when collaboration is most needed, enhancing efficiency and relevance.
- Utilize timeboxing for meetings to maintain focus and prevent unnecessary prolongation, allowing teams to conclude meetings once objectives are achieved.
- Facilitate frequent feedback loops through Sprint Reviews and Retrospectives to catch issues early and continuously improve team processes.

**For PM/PgM:** For Project/Program Managers in high-tech companies, adopting a 'just in time' approach to meetings can significantly enhance team productivity and focus. It ensures that meetings are purposeful and aligned with project goals, reducing time spent in unproductive discussions. This approach also supports agile transformations by fostering a culture of continuous improvement and responsive planning, which is crucial for successful project delivery and stakeholder satisfaction.
